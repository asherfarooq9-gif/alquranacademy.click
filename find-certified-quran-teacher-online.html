2

Step 3 — Search and Shortlist Academies

3

Use the right search terms and filter by key criteria

When searching for an online Quran teacher, use specific search terms based on your goal and location.

Effective search queries to use in Google:

  • "online Quran classes for kids USA" (or UK / Canada / Australia)
  • "learn Tajweed online 1-on-1"
  • "online Hifz program for children"
  • "female Quran teacher online"
  • "online Quran academy free trial"

Once you have a list of academies, filter them quickly by asking three questions:

  1. Have they been operating for 3+ years? Longevity signals stability and reliability.
  2. Do they have genuine, verifiable student reviews? Not just a star rating — look for specific testimonials from parents.
  3. Is teacher credential information publicly available or clearly shared on request? Transparency here is a green flag.

Step 4 — Verify Teacher Credentials

4

Ask the right questions before agreeing to anything

Once you have shortlisted 2–3 academies, contact each one and ask these specific questions about their teachers.

Questions to ask the academy:

  • "Where did your teachers complete their Quran education?"
  • "How many years of online teaching experience does this teacher have?"
  • "Do you have both male and female teachers available?"
  • "What is your process for vetting and selecting teachers?"
  • "What happens if my assigned teacher is unavailable?"
  • "Do you send monthly progress reports to parents?"

A confident, detailed answer to all of these is a strong positive signal. Vague or dismissive answers indicate a lack of structure and accountability.

Step 5 — Read Genuine Student Reviews

5

Look beyond star ratings — read for specifics

Star ratings alone can be misleading. A 4.8-star average from 12 reviews is far less reliable than a 4.9-star average from 900+ reviews. What matters is the content of the reviews.

When reading reviews, look for these specific indicators:

  • Mentions of teacher patience — especially important for young children
  • Specific progress milestones — "my son memorised Juz Amma in 6 months"
  • Long-term enrollment — reviews from families who have been with the academy for 1+ years
  • Mention of different countries — confirms the academy has global reach and timezone flexibility
  • Parent satisfaction with female teachers — important for Muslim families with daughters
⚠️ Watch out for: Generic 5-star reviews with no specific detail, reviews all posted within the same short window, or no reviews at all. These are signs of unverified or manufactured social proof.

Step 6 — Request a Free Trial Class

6

Never enroll without a trial — and never pay for the trial

This is the most important step. A free trial class removes all risk from the decision. If an academy will not offer a free trial, that tells you something important about their confidence in their own teachers.

What a free trial should include:

  • A full live session with the actual teacher who will be assigned to you — not a sales call
  • No credit card required whatsoever
  • No automatic billing after the trial
  • Time to ask the teacher questions directly

Step 7 — Evaluate and Enroll

7

Use these criteria to make your final decision

After the trial, take 10 minutes to evaluate what you observed before enrolling.

Use this checklist after the trial class:

  • ☐ Did the teacher explain clearly in English?
  • ☐ Were mistakes corrected patiently and kindly?
  • ☐ Did the student seem engaged and comfortable?
  • ☐ Was the session structured with a clear focus?
  • ☐ Did the teacher ask about the student's current level and goals?
  • ☐ Did you feel trust and confidence in the teacher?

If you can check most of these boxes, you have found the right teacher. Enroll, commit to a consistent schedule, and trust the process. Consistent attendance is the single biggest factor in how quickly a student progresses.
